# Reviewer Attacks

## Attack: This is contact anomaly detection with new labels.

Response: Partially answered. The paper distinguishes internal model adaptation from external-contact recovery, but the evidence is still a synthetic diagnostic.

## Attack: The rule depends on calibrated tactile residual features.

Response: Sustained and quantified by v2. At 0.7 shape-feature gain, causal F1 falls to 0.891 while the uncertainty gate remains 0.918.

## Attack: Surface compliance and object variation may collapse the feature separation.

Response: Sustained. The v2 stress shows that under-reported asymmetry, onset lag, or spatial jump can erase the clean advantage.

## Attack: The paper does not prove causality.

Response: Sustained. It proposes a causal diagnostic interface and tests a synthetic separation rule; it does not establish causal identification in hardware.
