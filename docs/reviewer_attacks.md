# Reviewer Attacks

## Attack: This is contact anomaly detection with new labels.

Response: The final paper distinguishes anomaly detection from response-source diagnosis. The output is not merely "surprising"; it selects model adaptation or external-contact recovery.

## Attack: The rule depends on calibrated tactile residual features.

Response: Correct. Calibration is part of the claim and is tested directly across seven calibration regimes. Severe attenuation, spatial blur, and cross-axis coupling are reported boundaries.

## Attack: Surface compliance and object variation may collapse the feature separation.

Response: The full-scale suite includes soft foam, cloth/rubber, low-friction transparent surfaces, compliance drift, and soft-object deformation. These factors reduce performance but do not erase the non-oracle margin in the generated suite.

## Attack: The paper does not prove causality.

Response: The paper claims controller-causal response diagnosis under an active contact graph, not causal discovery from arbitrary tactile streams.

## Attack: The oracle gap is large.

Response: The oracle gap is reported intentionally. It identifies future headroom while the proposed method remains the strongest non-oracle policy.
